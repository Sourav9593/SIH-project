Festival In Month Of January

Makar Sankranti

It is a Hindu festival that marks the sun's transition from the Southern to the Northern Hemisphere.

Makar Sankranti celebrations and kite flying are closely connected, as flying kites is thought to represent the sun rising and winter ending. Every year, on the 14th of January, India welcomes a wave of festive cheer across the country. Somewhere between hues of yellow and the blue sky enlivened with colourful kites, the country celebrates an end and a new beginning with Makar Sankranti the Harvest Festival.

Pongal

Pongal is a harvest festival celebrated by the Tamil community.

Pongal is a four-day festival celebrated by the Tamil community to thank the sun, nature, and farm animals for a bountiful harvest. The festival usually falls on January 14 or 15 and marks the beginning of the Tamil month of Thai. The festival is celebrated with different festivities on each day, including Bhogi, Thai Pongal, Mattu Pongal, and Kaanum Pongal.Pongal is also the name of a sweet dish made of boiled rice, milk, and jaggery that is prepared and eaten during the festival. The word pongal comes from the Tamil word pongu, which means "to boil over".

Lohri

Lohri is a popular winter Dogra and Punjabi folk festival celebrated primarily in Northern India.

Lohri is a harvest festival celebrated in parts of North India, including Punjab, Delhi, Haryana, and Himachal Pradesh. It's celebrated to mark the end of winter, the winter solstice, and the beginning of the harvest season for winter crops.People like to deck up in colourful traditional attires, sing and dance around the bonfire, welcoming the longer days of warmer temperature.
